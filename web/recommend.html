Pick your car — or scan a photo of it. This tool inspects its airflow and issues a verdict: what to bolt on, where it goes, what it costs, and when it starts paying you back. Every number is computed by an open, tested physics engine, never guessed.

Frontal area estimated per body-type fill factor (~0.78–0.82 × width × height). The modification physics is borrowed from your body type's archetype — only the dimensions are yours — so custom-car numbers carry an extra ±15% uncertainty, already folded into the error bars shown above.

STEP 1 — the photo tells us the shape. Upload a side-on photo. We place seven dots on your car; drag any that are off. Nothing leaves your device — this runs entirely in your browser.

STEP 2 — your numbers tell us the size. Fill in what you know from the car's spec sheet (a quick web search of your model). Wheelbase is enough; length and height make it sharper. Leave a box blank and we estimate it from the photo.

Why "give or take"? Our predictions land within about 10% on cars we can check, and each part's effect is only known as well as the wind-tunnel tests behind it. When the money comes back "between 11 months and 5 years", the slow end already assumes top price and the weak end of the physics.

The paperwork. Indian law says a registered car shouldn't differ from its papers. Wheel covers and a hidden floor panel change nothing on paper. Lips, skirts, spoilers and diffusers change the outside, so the RTO gets a say. We're engineers, not lawyers — check with your RTO, and fitting anything is your own risk.

Prices are a shopping budget, not a quote — Indian aftermarket ranges, fitted yourself. Your city will differ.

Measure it yourself. The project includes a road test: coast in neutral with a phone logging GPS speed, and read your car's real drag before and after the parts.
