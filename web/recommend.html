Why "give or take"? Because honest numbers come with wiggle room. Our predictions land within about 10% on cars we can check, and each part's effect is only known as well as wind-tunnel tests know it. When we say the money comes back "between 11 months and 5 years", the slow end already assumes you paid top price and got the weak end of the physics. No nasty surprises hiding in the fine print — this IS the fine print.

About the paperwork: Indian law says a registered car shouldn't be altered from what's on its papers. Covers that clip onto your wheels and a flat panel hidden under the floor change nothing on paper — that's why they're our headline picks. Anything that changes how the car looks from outside (lips, skirts, spoilers, diffusers) is where the RTO gets a say. We're engineers, not lawyers: double-check with your RTO before spending.

Prices are a shopping budget, not a quote — Indian aftermarket ranges, assuming you fit things yourself. Your city will differ.

Don't take our word for it. The same project includes a simple road test: coast in neutral with a phone logging GPS speed, and you can measure your car's real drag — before and after the parts — and catch us if we're wrong. That's the whole spirit of this tool.
